My grandmother kept her recipes in a Tupperware container she'd had since the seventies, and when she died last spring my aunt was the one who inherited it. I was a little jealous. I had asked, very politely, when I was about twelve, if I could have the rye bread recipe specifically, and she had laughed at me and said come back when you can be trusted with an oven. I never did go back. When I think of her now it's not the funerals or the holidays or any of that, it's specifically her kitchen, which always smelled like cardamom and dish soap in equal measure. I have tried to make her rye three times since the funeral, working from memory and one blurry photo my cousin texted me, and all three loaves have been somewhere between mediocre and inedible. I don't think it's the recipe I'm missing. I think it's her.
